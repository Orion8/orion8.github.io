The "Secular Cycle"

History breathes. According to Turchin's Demographic-Structural Theory, agrarian societies go through predictable oscillations driven by population pressure.

Integrative Phase: Population grows, prices are stable, and elites are unified.

Disintegrative Phase: Population outstrips resources (Malthusian Trap). Labor becomes cheap, elites overproduce, and state coffers drain, leading to collapse.

The Ratchet of Complexity

While individual empires fall, the human capacity for cooperation scales exponentially. This scatter plot tracks the territorial size of the world's largest empire at various points in history. Notice how the baseline rarely drops back to zero—a phenomenon known as the "Ratchet Effect."

The Universal Algorithm of Collapse

Ibn Khaldun described this cycle in the 14th century using the concept of Asabiyyah (social cohesion). A cohesive group establishes a state. Success brings luxury and inequality. The elite class expands faster than the economy can support ("Elite Overproduction"), leading to factionalism and eventual replacement by a new, more cohesive group.
